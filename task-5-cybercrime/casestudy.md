#### Case Study Report: UPI/Online Payment Fraud





##### Introduction



UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in which attackers trick users into authorizing payments. It mainly relies on social engineering rather than technical hacking.





##### Description of the Crime



In this type of fraud, scammers pretend to be genuine individuals such as bank officials, customer care agents, or buyers on online platforms. Their goal is to gain the victim’s trust and manipulate them into making a payment.





##### How the Fraud Happens



The fraudster contacts the victim through a call, SMS, or apps and creates urgency, such as offering a refund or confirming a purchase. They then send a UPI collect request or QR code. The victim is asked to enter their UPI PIN, thinking they will receive money. However, entering the PIN actually authorizes a payment to the fraudster.





##### Targeted Victims



College students, elderly individuals, and online sellers on platforms like OLX are common targets due to lack of awareness.





##### Consequences



Victims may face financial loss, stress, and difficulty in recovering money if the fraud is not reported quickly.





##### Conclusion



UPI fraud highlights the importance of digital awareness. According to Reserve Bank of India, users should never share their UPI PIN or approve unknown requests. Staying cautious can help prevent such cybercrimes.
